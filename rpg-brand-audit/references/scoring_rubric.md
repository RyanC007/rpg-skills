# Brand 360 Audit — Scoring Rubric

Use this rubric to assign a score of 1-5 to each of the five audit areas. Apply the score that best matches the majority of criteria observed. When in doubt, score down.

---

## Area 1: Website and Digital Presence

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **5** | Value proposition is clear above the fold in under 5 seconds. CTAs are specific, visible, and present on every major page. Site is fully mobile-responsive. Pages load in under 2 seconds. Content hierarchy is logical with clear H1/H2 structure. Service or product pages are detailed and descriptive. |
| **4** | Value proposition is clear but may require slight scrolling. CTAs are present but could be stronger or more specific. Site is mobile-responsive with minor issues. Load speed is acceptable. Most pages have good content structure. |
| **3** | Value proposition is present but vague or buried. CTAs exist but are generic ("Contact Us," "Learn More"). Site is functional on mobile but has layout issues. Load speed is average. Content structure is inconsistent across pages. |
| **2** | Value proposition is unclear or missing. CTAs are absent on key pages. Mobile experience is poor. Load speed is slow. Content is thin or outdated on multiple pages. |
| **1** | No clear value proposition. No CTAs. Site is broken or unusable on mobile. Load speed is very slow. Content is severely lacking or inaccurate. |

---

## Area 2: Brand Voice and Messaging

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **5** | Messaging is consistent across website and all social channels. Tone is clearly defined and speaks directly to the ideal customer. Copy is free of jargon and vague claims. The brand sounds like one person across every touchpoint. |
| **4** | Messaging is mostly consistent with minor variations across channels. Tone is generally clear. Occasional jargon or generic phrases present but not dominant. |
| **3** | Messaging is inconsistent between website and social channels. Tone shifts noticeably. Some jargon or vague marketing language present. The ideal customer is referenced but not spoken to directly. |
| **2** | Messaging is significantly inconsistent. Tone is unclear or generic. Heavy use of jargon or unprovable claims. The ideal customer is not clearly identified in the copy. |
| **1** | No discernible brand voice. Messaging is contradictory, absent, or entirely generic. Copy reads like a template with no brand personality. |

---

## Area 3: SEO and Search Visibility

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **5** | Brand ranks in positions 1-5 for at least 3 of the 5 target keywords. Appears in the Local Pack (if applicable). Google Business Profile is complete and active. Meta titles and descriptions are present and keyword-relevant on all major pages. H1 tags are present and descriptive. |
| **4** | Brand ranks in positions 6-15 for most target keywords. Local Pack presence is partial or inconsistent. GBP is mostly complete. Meta titles are present but descriptions may be missing or generic on some pages. |
| **3** | Brand ranks in positions 16-20 for some keywords, or "Not in top 20" for most. Local Pack presence is absent or inaccurate. GBP exists but is incomplete. Meta titles and descriptions are missing or generic on many pages. |
| **2** | Brand does not appear in the top 20 for most target keywords. No Local Pack presence. GBP is missing or unclaimed. Meta titles are missing or auto-generated on most pages. |
| **1** | Brand does not appear in the top 20 for any target keyword. No GBP. No meta optimization. No visible on-page SEO effort. |

---

## Area 4: Social Media Presence

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **5** | All relevant profiles are complete with branded visuals, bio, and website link. Posts at least 3-4 times per week. Content mix is balanced (60-70% value/educational, 30-40% promotional). Posts receive consistent engagement. Brand responds to comments. |
| **4** | Most profiles are complete. Posts 1-2 times per week. Content mix is mostly value-driven. Some engagement present. Occasional response to comments. |
| **3** | Profiles exist but are partially complete (missing bio, link, or branded visuals). Posts 2-4 times per month. Content is mostly promotional. Engagement is low. Comments rarely addressed. |
| **2** | Profiles exist but are largely incomplete. Posts less than once per month or posting is erratic. Content is entirely promotional or off-brand. No meaningful engagement. |
| **1** | No social profiles, or profiles exist but have not been posted to in 6+ months. Profiles are unbranded or contain outdated information. |

---

## Area 5: Competitive Position

| Score | Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **5** | Brand outranks or matches top competitors on at least 3 of 5 target keywords. Clear, differentiated positioning that competitors do not replicate. Brand occupies content gaps that competitors are missing. |
| **4** | Brand ranks comparably to competitors on most keywords. Positioning is differentiated but not fully communicated. Some content gaps identified and partially addressed. |
| **3** | Brand is outranked by most competitors on target keywords. Positioning is similar to competitors with no clear differentiator. Content gaps exist and are not being addressed. |
| **2** | Brand is significantly outranked by all top competitors on all target keywords. No discernible positioning differentiation. Competitors are clearly winning the content and visibility battle. |
| **1** | Brand has no visible competitive presence in search. Competitors dominate all target keywords. Brand positioning is absent or indistinguishable from competitors. |
