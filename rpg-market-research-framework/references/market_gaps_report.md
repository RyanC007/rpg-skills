# Ready, Plan, Grow! Market Gaps Report
**Date:** March 8, 2026
**Purpose:** Identify unmet needs in the AI-for-small-business education market to sharpen positioning for the $50/month RPG community.
**Sources:** Reddit, YouTube, Amazon book reviews, LinkedIn, G2, Trustpilot, Quora, HBR

---

## Executive Summary

The AI-for-business education market is crowded with tools, courses, and communities. But the data from across every source tells the same story: people are not getting what they actually need. The gap is not awareness. It is not access to tools. The gap is a trusted guide who will give them a simple starting point and stay with them through implementation. That is the space RPG occupies.

---

## The 10 Market Gaps

### Gap 1: A Starting Point, Not a Strategy

Every program sells strategy. Nobody sells a clear "do this first, then this" sequence. People do not want a framework to study. They want to be told what to do on Monday morning. The phrase "I don't know where to start" appears across every single source pulled in this research. Nobody is answering that question simply and directly.

**Trigger phrases from the market:**
- "I don't know where to start"
- "I don't have time to experiment"
- "They have a document full of aspirations and no idea what to actually do on Monday morning"
- "You don't need a strategy. You need a starting point."

**RPG opportunity:** Lead with a clear, numbered sequence. The first thing a new member gets is not a library of content. It is a "do this first" plan.

---

### Gap 2: Support for True Beginners

Existing programs assume the buyer already runs a business with systems in place. Trustpilot reviews of competitor programs make this explicit: "It's maybe only good for people who have a marketing business running. But someone who is new to this... stay away." The person who is brand new, with no systems and no AI experience, has almost nowhere to go that is not too technical, too expensive, or too vague.

**Trigger phrases from the market:**
- "Tutorials assume you already know the basics"
- "New to this and feel abandoned"
- "Someone who is new to this... stay away"
- "I thought AI was only for big tech companies"

**RPG opportunity:** Position explicitly as the program for people who are starting from zero. Not "level up your AI skills." Instead: "Start here, even if you've never used AI in your business."

---

### Gap 3: Consistency, Not Just Capability

People try AI, get inconsistent results, and give up. The problem is not that they cannot use the tools. It is that every team member starts from zero every time. There is no shared system, no shared prompts, no shared brand voice. Nobody is selling a consistency system at an SMB price point. RPG's proven frameworks solve exactly this problem and it is not being communicated in the market.

**Trigger phrases from the market:**
- "Everyone is starting from zero. Every single morning."
- "Half my team writes prompts like 'make it better' and wonders why the output sucks"
- "More mental load. More inconsistency. More frustration."
- "AI without good systems doesn't help much. AI plus good systems = hours and hours cut down."

**RPG opportunity:** Sell the consistency outcome explicitly. "Your whole team gets the same result, every time."

---

### Gap 4: Real Support and Accountability

The top complaint across G2 and Trustpilot is not about the content quality. It is about what happens after purchase. "The product is fantastic, the support is way behind what should be expected." People buy communities and courses and then feel abandoned. A $50/month community with genuine weekly touchpoints, live calls, and accountability is the direct answer to this complaint.

**Trigger phrases from the market:**
- "Never got any support for anything except them asking for more money"
- "The support is way behind what should be expected"
- "I was already skeptical and thought it might be a scam"
- "Felt abandoned after the first week"

**RPG opportunity:** Make support and accountability the headline of the offer, not a footnote. "Weekly calls. Real answers. We stay with you."

---

### Gap 5: Practical Over Theoretical

Every source uses the word "practical." Amazon reviews, Reddit, YouTube comments. People are drowning in theory, frameworks, and strategy decks. They want to see AI applied to a real business like theirs, not a Fortune 500 case study or a tech startup example.

**Trigger phrases from the market:**
- "Practical, straightforward, and instantly useful"
- "A very real and practical approach"
- "Real-world examples"
- "No jargon. No tech talk. No corporate buzzwords."

**RPG opportunity:** Every piece of content and every framework should be anchored to a real small business example. Not hypothetical. Not enterprise. A business owner who looks like the buyer.

---

### Gap 6: Industry-Specific Guidance

Every AI program is generic. A plumber, a coach, and a restaurant owner all get the same content. Nobody is saying "here is exactly how a service business uses AI differently from a product business." People want to see their specific business type reflected back at them.

**Trigger phrases from the market:**
- "I don't know if this applies to my kind of business"
- "All the examples are for tech companies"
- "What does this actually look like for a [gym / salon / consultant]"

**RPG opportunity:** Build vertical-specific content tracks or case studies. Even a simple library of "AI for [industry]" examples would be a significant differentiator.

---

### Gap 7: Team Implementation, Not Just Solo Use

Almost all AI education is built for the solo founder. But most small businesses have 2 to 10 people. The problem is not the owner not knowing how to use AI. It is the whole team using it inconsistently. Nobody is selling "AI for your whole team" at an SMB price point.

**Trigger phrases from the market:**
- "My team keeps getting different results"
- "Everyone is doing it differently"
- "How do I get my team actually using this"

**RPG opportunity:** Frame the community as a resource for the whole business, not just the owner. "Bring your team. Build shared systems."

---

### Gap 8: The "After the Tool" Problem

There are thousands of resources on which AI tools to use. There is almost nothing on what happens after setup. Maintenance, updating prompts, handling when outputs drift, keeping content on-brand over time. People buy the tool, set it up once, and then it slowly stops working. Nobody teaches the ongoing management layer.

**Trigger phrases from the market:**
- "It worked for a week and then the outputs got worse"
- "I don't know how to keep it on brand"
- "Who maintains this after it's set up"

**RPG opportunity:** A "maintenance and refresh" module or monthly prompt audit would be a unique offering with no direct competitor.

---

### Gap 9: ROI Clarity

Business owners want to justify the time and money spent on AI. Nobody is giving them a simple way to measure whether AI is actually saving them time or generating revenue. A simple AI ROI tracker or a framework for measuring impact would be a powerful differentiator and a strong lead magnet.

**Trigger phrases from the market:**
- "I don't know if it's actually saving me time"
- "How do I know if this is working"
- "I need to show my business partner it's worth it"

**RPG opportunity:** Build and publish a simple "AI ROI Calculator" as a free tool or lead magnet. It solves a real problem and captures the exact buyer.

---

### Gap 10: The "I Tried It and It Failed" Re-Entry Point

A large segment has already tried AI, been burned, and walked away. They are not beginners. They are skeptics with earned experience. Nobody is speaking directly to them with a message that says "here is why it failed and here is what you do differently." This is one of the highest-intent audiences in the market and it is completely unaddressed.

**Trigger phrases from the market:**
- "We invested in AI, hired someone to set it up, bought all the right tools. Six months later nothing meaningful changed."
- "His scepticism wasn't fear. It was earned experience."
- "I don't know if the problem is the tool, the team, or the strategy"
- "AI on top of a broken process just gives you broken outputs faster"

**RPG opportunity:** This is the single biggest untapped audience. A specific campaign or landing page targeting "I tried AI and it didn't work" would convert at a very high rate because the buyer already believes in the problem. They just need a credible reason to try again.
